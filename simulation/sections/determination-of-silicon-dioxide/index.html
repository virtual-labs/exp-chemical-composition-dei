c) Determination of Silicon Dioxide (SiO₂)

Section Overview:

This section focuses on the quantitative determination of silicon dioxide (SiO₂) content in the cement sample. The process involves acidifying the filtrate with nitric acid, evaporating to dryness to convert silica into insoluble SiO₂, followed by filtration, washing, drying, and weighing to calculate the SiO₂ percentage.

Key Chemical Principle: Silicon dioxide becomes insoluble when the solution is acidified and evaporated to dryness, allowing for its separation and quantification.

Chemical Process: SiO₃²⁻ + 2H⁺ → SiO₂(s) + H₂O (on evaporation)

Step 1: Acidifying with Nitric Acid

Add dilute nitric acid (HNO₃) to a portion of the filtrate to acidify the solution and prepare for silica precipitation.

Start Step 1

Step 2: Evaporation to Dryness

Evaporate the acidified solution to dryness to convert dissolved silica into insoluble silicon dioxide (SiO₂).

Start Step 2

Step 3: Filtration and Washing

Filter the residue to separate insoluble SiO₂ and wash thoroughly to remove any soluble impurities.

Start Step 3

Step 4: Drying and Weighing

Dry the silicon dioxide residue and weigh it to calculate the percentage of SiO₂ in the cement sample.
