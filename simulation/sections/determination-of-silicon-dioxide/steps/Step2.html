Step 2: Evaporation to Dryness

About this step:

In this step, we will evaporate the acidified solution to complete dryness by gentle heating. This process converts the dissolved silicic acid into insoluble silicon dioxide (SiO‚ÇÇ).

During evaporation, the water is removed and the silicic acid undergoes dehydration to form amorphous silica (SiO‚ÇÇ). This transformation is crucial because it converts the dissolved silicon compounds into an insoluble form that can be filtered and weighed.

The heating must be gentle and controlled to prevent spattering and ensure complete dehydration without decomposing other components. The residue will contain silicon dioxide along with some other insoluble materials.
