b) Determination of Calcium Oxide (CaO)

Step 3c: Drying the Precipitate

About this step:

In this step, we will dry the washed calcium oxalate precipitate in an oven at 110°C to remove all moisture and water of crystallization.

Drying at 110°C is critical because it removes residual water without decomposing the calcium oxalate. This temperature is sufficient to eliminate moisture while maintaining the integrity of the CaC₂O₄·H₂O structure needed for accurate quantitative analysis.

The precipitate will be dried to constant mass, meaning successive weighings will show no significant change, indicating complete removal of water.
