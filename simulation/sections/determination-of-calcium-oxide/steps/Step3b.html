b) Determination of Calcium Oxide (CaO)

Step 3b: Washing the Precipitate

About this step:

In this step, we will wash the filtered calcium oxalate precipitate with distilled water to remove any soluble impurities, excess reagents, or ammonium salts that may have adhered to the precipitate.

Washing is crucial because any remaining ammonium compounds or other soluble impurities would interfere with the accurate determination of calcium content. We use distilled water to ensure no additional ions are introduced.

The precipitate on the filter paper will be washed several times with small portions of distilled water until the washings are free from ammonium ions.

Click on the wash bottle to wash the precipitate with distilled water.
