#### Preparation of Cement 
Step 1: Click on the trowel to transfer cement onto the crucible.<br><img src="procedure_images/a.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>

Step 2: Click on the towel to weigh 1 gram of cement accurately using an analytical balance.<br><img src="procedure_images/b.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 3: Click on the crucible to transfer the cement sample to a beaker.<br><img src="procedure_images/c.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 4: Click on the HCl bottle to add 20 mL of dilute hydrochloric acid (HCl) to the beaker.<br><img src="procedure_images/d.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 5: Click on the beaker to heat the solution gently.<br><img src="procedure_images/e.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 6: Click on the Funnel to filter the solution to remove any undissolved particles.<br><img src="procedure_images/f.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 7: Click on the measuring cylinder to transfer the filtered solution to a clean container for further analysis.<br><img src="procedure_images/g.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
#### Preparation of Calcium Oxide 
Step 1: Click on the pipette to add ammonium hydroxide (NH₄OH) dropwise to the filtrate until 
The solution is slightly alkaline (pH approximately 8–9).<br><img src="procedure_images/1.1.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 2: Click on the measuring cylinder to add 10 mL of ammonium oxalate ((NH₄)₂C₂O₄) solution to 
precipitate calcium oxalate.<br><img src="procedure_images/1.2.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 3:
•	Click on the filter paper to filter the precipitate.<br><img src="procedure_images/1.3.1.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the knob of the oven to open it.<br><img src="procedure_images/1.3.2.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the crucible to dry it in an oven at 110°C.<br><img src="procedure_images/1.3.3.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the screen to set the temperature to 110°C to dry.<br><img src="procedure_images/1.3.4.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 

Step 4: 
•	Click on the crucible to weigh the dried precipitate and calculate the percentage of CaO.<br><img src="procedure_images/1.4.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the calculate CaO % to calculate the percentage of CaO.<br><img src="procedure_images/1.4.2.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Preparation of Iron Oxide and Aluminum Oxide
Step 1: Click on the pipette to another portion of the filtrate, and add ammonium hydroxide (NH₄OH)until a precipitate forms.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.1.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 2: Click on the filter paper to filter the precipitate, which contains ferric hydroxide [Fe(OH)₃] and aluminum hydroxide [Al(OH)₃].<br><img src="procedure_images/2.2.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 3: 
•	Click on the measuring cylinder to dissolve the precipitate in dilute hydrochloric acid (HCl).<br><img src="procedure_images/2.3.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the crucible to dissolve the precipitate in dilute hydrochloric acid (HCl).<br><img src="procedure_images/2.3.2.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 4: Click on the pipette to add a few drops of nitric acid (HNO₃) to the solution.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.4.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 5:	Click on the pipette to add dilute sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) to provide the required acidic medium.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.5.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the beaker to transfer the solution to a conical flask and titrate with potassium
permanganate (KMnO₄) to determine iron oxide (Fe₂O₃).<br><img src="procedure_images/2.5.2.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the knob to add The prepared solution in the conical flask is titrated with standard potassiumpermanganate (KMnO₄) solution.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.5.3.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the knob to add. The titration is continued until a permanent light pink colour appears, indicating the end point. The KMnO₄ volume is noted to determine the Fe₂O₃% in the cement sample.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.5.4.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the calculate of Fe₂O₃% to calculate the percentage of Fe₂O₃%.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.5.5.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
Step 6: 
Click on the burette to add the Sodium hydroxide (NaOH) solution, which is used to titrate the aluminium-containing solution.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the beaker to see aluminium react with sodium hydroxide to form aluminium hydroxide precipitate initially, which then dissolves in excess alkali, forming soluble aluminate.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.2.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the filter paper to filter the precipitate.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.3.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the filter paper to filter the precipitate.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.4.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the knob of the oven to open it.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.5.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the crucible to dry it in an oven at 110°C.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.6.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the oven screen and set the temperature to 110°C to dry.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.7.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
 
•	Click on the crucible to weigh the dried precipitate and use the titration data to calculate the percentage of aluminium oxide (Al₂O₃) present in the cement sample.<br><img src="procedure_images/2.6.8.png" alt="procedure" width="500"><br>
