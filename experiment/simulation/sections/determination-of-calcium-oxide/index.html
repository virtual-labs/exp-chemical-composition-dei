A) Determination of Calcium Oxide (CaO)

Section Overview:

This section focuses on the quantitative determination of calcium oxide (CaO) content in the cement sample. The process involves precipitating calcium as calcium oxalate (CaC₂O₄), drying the precipitate, weighing it, and calculating the CaO percentage using stoichiometric relationships.

Key Chemical Principle: Calcium ions react with oxalate ions to form calcium oxalate precipitate, which is then converted stoichiometrically to determine the original CaO content.

Chemical Reaction: Ca²⁺ + C₂O₄²⁻ → CaC₂O₄ ↓

Please complete Step before proceeding.

Step 1: Adding Ammonium Hydroxide

Add NH₄OH dropwise to the filtrate until the solution becomes slightly alkaline (pH ~8-9). This step precipitates interfering metal ions.

Start Step 1

Step 2: Adding Ammonium Oxalate

Add (NH₄)₂C₂O₄ solution to precipitate calcium ions as calcium oxalate (CaC₂O₄).

Start Step 2

Step 3: Filtering and Drying

Filter the calcium oxalate precipitate and dry it completely to remove water content.

Start Step 3

Step 4: Weighing and Calculation

Weigh the dried precipitate and calculate the percentage of CaO using stoichiometric relationships (Result: 64.0% CaO).

Start Step 4
