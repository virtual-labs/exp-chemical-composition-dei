Determination of Magnesium Oxide

Section Overview:

This section focuses on the quantitative determination of magnesium oxide in cement. Magnesium ions are precipitated as magnesium ammonium phosphate, dried, and weighed to calculate MgO percentage.

Key Chemical Principle: Magnesium reacts with ammonium phosphate in alkaline medium to form insoluble magnesium ammonium phosphate (MgNH₄PO₄), which can be weighed to estimate MgO content.

Chemical Reaction: Mg²⁺ + NH₄⁺ + HPO₄²⁻ → MgNH₄PO₄ ↓

Please complete Step before proceeding.

Step 1: Addition of Reagent

Diammonium hydrogen phosphate is added to the filtrate containing magnesium ions, forming a white precipitate of magnesium ammonium phosphate.

Start Step 1

Step 2: Filtration of Precipitate

The precipitate is filtered using filter paper to separate solid MgNH₄PO₄ from the liquid filtrate.

Start Step 2

Step 3: Drying the Precipitate

The crucible containing the precipitate is placed in a hot air oven. The remaining filtrate obtained after the removal of iron (Fe) and aluminium (Al) oxides in earlier parts of the analysis is taken for the determination of magnesium oxide (MgO).

Start Step 3

Step 4: Weighing and Calculation

The residue is weighed accurately to determine the magnesium oxide content and further calculations are carried out.

Start Step 4
