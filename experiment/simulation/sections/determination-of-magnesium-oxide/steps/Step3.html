e) Determination of Magnesium Oxide (MgO)

Step 3: Drying the Precipitate

About this step:

In this step, we will dry the magnesium ammonium phosphate precipitate in an oven at 110°C to remove all moisture and water of crystallization.

Drying at 110°C is critical because it removes residual water without decomposing the ppt.Drying ensures accurate weighing of the residue, as any remaining water could lead to errors in mass and hence in the calculation of MgO percentage.

The precipitate will be dried to constant mass, meaning successive weighings will show no significant change, indicating complete removal of water.
