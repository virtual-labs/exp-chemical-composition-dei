d) Determination of Iron Oxide (Fe₂O₃) and Aluminium Oxide (Al₂O₃)

Section Overview:

This section explains the quantitative determination of iron oxide and aluminium oxide in cement. The process involves selective precipitation of Fe³⁺ and Al³⁺ as hydroxides, re-dissolving them, and then using separate titrations to determine Fe₂O₃ and Al₂O₃ content.

Key Chemical Principle: Fe³⁺ ions form Fe(OH)₃ precipitate with NH₄OH, which can be titrated with KMnO₄ in acidic medium
Al³⁺ ions form Al(OH)₃, which can be further analyzed by reaction with NaOH.

Chemical Process: Fe³⁺ + 3OH⁻ → Fe(OH)₃ ↓
5Fe²⁺ + MnO₄⁻ + 8H⁺ → 5Fe³⁺ + Mn²⁺ + 4H₂O
Al³⁺ + 3OH⁻ → Al(OH)₃ ↓

Step 1: Take Filtrate sample and Add NH₄OH

Taking a portion of filtrate and add ammonium hydroxide (NH₄OH) until a brownish-red precipitate appears.

Start Step 1

Step 2: Filter the hydroxides

The mixed hydroxide precipitate is separated by filtration.Needed to isolate Fe and Al compounds for further testing.

Start Step 2

Step 3: To dissolve in dilute HCL

The precipitate in the beaker is dissolved in dilute HCl to get a clear solution containing Fe³⁺ and Al³⁺ ions.

Start Step 3

Step 4: Oxidation Medium Preparation

To the solution containing Fe³⁺ and Al³⁺ (from the precipitate dissolved in HCl), add concentrated HNO₃.

Start Step 4

Step 5: Titration of Fe²⁺ with KMnO₄

KMnO₄ titration must be carried out in strongly acidic conditions, and H₂SO₄ is the best acid for this because it does not interfere with the redox reaction.

Start Step 5

Step 6: Addition of NaOH to obtain aluminium oxide

Sodium hydroxide is added to the filtrate to precipitate aluminum as white gelatinous aluminum hydroxide..
